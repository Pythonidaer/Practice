Started From the Bottom

When I began working in restaurants, I was getting underpaid. I worked hard, but came to learn that the restaurant industry wasn't my passion. It wasn't meant for people in their twenties, not unless they were running the place. The pay was ludacrous! However, I lacked skills to get employed elsewhere.

That's when I went to college.

Luckily, financial aid covered most of my two years at Community College. Unfortunately, when I graduated I still had no idea what I wanted to do for a career. I began working odd jobs in sales, numerous brand representations (it's called being a Brand Ambassador in the gig economy), and eventually I got my first big break.
